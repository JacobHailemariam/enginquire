Pre-Calc Review — Formula Sheet

The formulas allowed on the Math 30-1 diploma exam (permutations & combinations excluded).

Laws of Logarithms

$\log_b(MN) = \log_b M + \log_b N$
$\log_b\!\left(\dfrac{M}{N}\right) = \log_b M - \log_b N$
$\log_b(M^n) = n\,\log_b M$
$\log_b c = \dfrac{\log_a c}{\log_a b}$  (change of base)

Transformations & Growth

General transformed function: $\;y = a\,f\!\big[b(x - h)\big] + k$
Growth / decay: $\;y = a\,b^{\frac{t}{p}}$

Trigonometry — Ratios

$\tan\theta = \dfrac{\sin\theta}{\cos\theta} \qquad \cot\theta = \dfrac{\cos\theta}{\sin\theta}$
$\csc\theta = \dfrac{1}{\sin\theta} \qquad \sec\theta = \dfrac{1}{\cos\theta} \qquad \cot\theta = \dfrac{1}{\tan\theta}$

Trigonometry — Identities

$\sin^2\theta + \cos^2\theta = 1$
$1 + \tan^2\theta = \sec^2\theta \qquad 1 + \cot^2\theta = \csc^2\theta$
$\sin(2\theta) = 2\sin\theta\cos\theta$
$\cos(2\theta) = \cos^2\theta - \sin^2\theta = 2\cos^2\theta - 1 = 1 - 2\sin^2\theta$
$\sin(\alpha\pm\beta) = \sin\alpha\cos\beta \pm \cos\alpha\sin\beta$
$\cos(\alpha\pm\beta) = \cos\alpha\cos\beta \mp \sin\alpha\sin\beta$

Binomial Theorem

General term: $\;t_{k+1} = \dbinom{n}{k}\,x^{\,n-k}\,y^{\,k}$

Calculus Review — Formula Sheet

Trimmed to exactly what the calculus review test needs.

Differentiation

First principles: $\;f'(x)=\displaystyle\lim_{h\to 0}\dfrac{f(x+h)-f(x)}{h}$
Power rule: $\;\dfrac{d}{dx}x^{n}=n\,x^{\,n-1}$
Product rule: $\;\dfrac{d}{dx}\big[f\,g\big]=f'g+g'f$
Quotient rule: $\;\dfrac{d}{dx}\!\left[\dfrac{f}{g}\right]=\dfrac{f'g-g'f}{g^{2}}$
Chain rule: $\;\dfrac{d}{dx}f\big(g(x)\big)=f'\big(g(x)\big)\,g'(x)$
Chain (power): $\;\dfrac{d}{dx}\big[g(x)\big]^{n}=n\big[g(x)\big]^{n-1}g'(x)$

Integration

$\displaystyle\int a\,x^{n}\,dx=\dfrac{a\,x^{\,n+1}}{n+1}+C,\quad n\neq -1$

Geometry — for related rates

Sphere: $\;V=\dfrac{4}{3}\pi r^{3}, \quad SA=4\pi r^{2}$
Cube: $\;V=s^{3} \qquad$ Cylinder: $\;V=\pi r^{2}h$
Triangle: $\;A=\dfrac{1}{2}\,b\,h$

Algebra

Line: $\;m(x-x_{1})=y-y_{1}$
Quadratic: $\;x=\dfrac{-b\pm\sqrt{b^{2}-4ac}}{2a}$
